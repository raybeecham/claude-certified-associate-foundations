# Before You Publish

Use this before sending, posting, presenting, or handing off AI-assisted work.

## Content quality

- [ ] The output answers the actual business question.
- [ ] Required sections and fields are present.
- [ ] Claims are supported by authorized evidence.
- [ ] Facts, assumptions, inference, and opinion are distinguishable.
- [ ] Dates, names, units, and figures are checked.
- [ ] Calculations match executed and reconciled results.
- [ ] Uncertainty and missing evidence are visible.

## Source integrity

- [ ] Primary sources are preferred where appropriate.
- [ ] Source versions and dates are current.
- [ ] Citations resolve to the claimed evidence.
- [ ] Conflicting evidence is disclosed rather than silently merged.
- [ ] No fabricated source, quote, or statistic remains.

## Audience and usability

- [ ] Tone and detail fit the intended audience.
- [ ] The document makes the required decision or next action clear.
- [ ] Important limitations are not buried.
- [ ] Accessibility and formatting requirements are met.
- [ ] The final file opens and renders correctly.

## Privacy and security

- [ ] Unauthorized or unnecessary sensitive data is removed.
- [ ] Hidden fields, comments, metadata, and attachments are reviewed.
- [ ] Credentials, secrets, and internal identifiers are absent.
- [ ] The recipient list and sharing permissions are correct.
- [ ] Public-safe examples do not identify nonpublic work.

## Human review

- [ ] A qualified reviewer checked consequential claims.
- [ ] Reviewer changes and overrides are documented where required.
- [ ] Final approval is recorded.
- [ ] AI assistance is disclosed when policy or context requires it.

## Release record

Record:

- deliverable version;
- source set and dates;
- model and prompt version when relevant;
- validation performed;
- reviewer and approval date;
- known limitations; and
- correction or withdrawal process.

## Final question

> Would I be comfortable explaining how every consequential claim was produced and verified?
