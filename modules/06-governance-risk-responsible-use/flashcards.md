# Flashcards: Governance, Risk & Responsible Use

Use active recall. Read the front, answer aloud, then open the back.

## Card 1

**Front:** What should happen before confidential data is sent to Claude?

<details>
<summary>Back</summary>

Classify the data and verify the environment, terms, access, retention, logging, residency, and organizational approval.

</details>

## Card 2

**Front:** What is a high-impact use?

<details>
<summary>Back</summary>

A use that can materially affect rights, access, opportunities, safety, finances, health, education, housing, or legal position.

</details>

## Card 3

**Front:** What makes a human-in-the-loop control substantive?

<details>
<summary>Back</summary>

Expertise, access to evidence, decision authority, adequate time, and placement before the consequential action.

</details>

## Card 4

**Front:** What is prompt injection?

<details>
<summary>Back</summary>

Untrusted content designed to alter behavior, override authority, disclose data, or induce unauthorized actions.

</details>

## Card 5

**Front:** What system controls reduce prompt-injection risk?

<details>
<summary>Back</summary>

Authority separation, least-privilege tools, validation, sandboxing, allowlists, approval gates, and monitoring.

</details>

## Card 6

**Front:** What should be reviewed before enabling a third-party skill or connector?

<details>
<summary>Back</summary>

Provenance, code, permissions, network and file access, credentials, updates, retention, dependencies, and incident support.

</details>

## Card 7

**Front:** Why minimize logs?

<details>
<summary>Back</summary>

Logs can become a secondary sensitive-data store and increase privacy and breach impact.

</details>

## Card 8

**Front:** Who can approve a governance exception?

<details>
<summary>Back</summary>

A named accountable human authority through a documented exception process.

</details>

## Card 9

**Front:** What should a change review cover?

<details>
<summary>Back</summary>

Model, prompt, tools, permissions, sources, users, data, review process, policy, monitoring, and rollback.

</details>

## Card 10

**Front:** How should a prohibited request be handled?

<details>
<summary>Back</summary>

Refuse the prohibited action and provide a safe permitted alternative or escalation when appropriate.

</details>

## Card 11

**Front:** What is the first priority after suspected exfiltration?

<details>
<summary>Back</summary>

Contain the affected workflow and permissions while preserving evidence.

</details>

## Card 12

**Front:** What does accountable AI use preserve?

<details>
<summary>Back</summary>

Human and organizational responsibility for decisions, controls, monitoring, and remediation.

</details>
