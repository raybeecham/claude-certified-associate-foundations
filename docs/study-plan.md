# Four-Week Study Plan

This plan assumes roughly **45 to 75 minutes on weekdays** and **two to three hours on one weekend day**. Adjust the cadence without changing the learning sequence.

## Week 1: Platform and prompting

### Day 1

- Read the certification overview.
- Establish a baseline with 10 mixed CLI questions.
- Create a weak-area log.

### Days 2 and 3

- Complete Module 1 notes.
- Build the platform-selection matrix lab.
- Drill Module 1 flashcards.
- Take the Module 1 quiz.

### Days 4 and 5

- Complete Module 2 notes.
- Rewrite the prompt-clinic examples.
- Compare prompts against explicit success criteria.
- Take the Module 2 quiz.

### Weekend checkpoint

- Retake missed questions without notes.
- Explain context, output limits, state, system instructions, examples, delimiters, structured outputs, and tool contracts aloud.
- Target: at least 75% on Domains 1 and 2.

## Week 2: Evaluation and solution design

### Days 1 and 2

- Complete Module 3.
- Build the grounded-output evaluation rubric.
- Practice choosing code-based, human, and model-assisted graders.

### Days 3 through 5

- Complete Module 4.
- Diagram the governed workflow lab.
- Mark each step as model, deterministic code, tool, data store, or human decision.

### Weekend checkpoint

- Complete Case Study 1.
- Take 14 mixed questions from Domains 1 through 4.
- Target: at least 80% on Domains 1 through 4.

## Week 3: Configuration, governance, and operations

### Days 1 and 2

- Complete Module 5.
- Build a knowledge-source register.
- Practice resolving source conflicts and stale content.

### Days 3 and 4

- Complete Module 6.
- Threat-model the secure policy assistant.
- Review data classification, least privilege, human oversight, prompt injection, logging, and incident response.

### Day 5

- Complete Module 7.
- Use the troubleshooting decision tree on three synthetic failures.

### Weekend checkpoint

- Complete Case Studies 2 and 3.
- Take 21 mixed questions across all domains.
- Target: at least 80% overall and no unresolved governance-critical misses.

## Week 4: Integration and exam readiness

### Day 1

- Review the master cheat sheet.
- Drill all missed flashcards.

### Day 2

- Take Practice Exam 1 under timed, closed-note conditions.
- Classify every miss: knowledge, reading, judgment, or avoidable error.

### Day 3

- Remediate the weakest two domains.
- Reperform the corresponding labs.

### Day 4

- Take Practice Exam 2 under the same conditions.
- Compare error patterns, not just total scores.

### Day 5

- Verify volatile facts against current official sources.
- Review governance and troubleshooting decision paths.
- Stop adding new material.

### Final checkpoint

Recommended readiness indicators:

- 80% or better on both practice exams;
- no recurring misses involving data protection, source grounding, high-impact decisions, or tool authorization;
- ability to explain each domain without notes; and
- ability to defend why a chosen design is simpler and safer than its alternatives.

These are study recommendations, not official certification requirements.
